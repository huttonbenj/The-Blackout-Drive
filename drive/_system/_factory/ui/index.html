BLACKOUT PROTOCOL

The toggle in the top-right corner of the screen. This is your one-click security switch.

When Blackout Protocol is ON (the default):
• Network Lock is forced ON — the drive cannot access the internet for any reason
• Encrypt Chat History is forced ON — your conversations are encrypted with your master password

You can turn it OFF to control these settings individually in the Settings panel. Turning it off does not make you less secure — both settings stay on until you manually change them. You can turn Blackout Protocol back on at any time.

Note: Blackout Protocol also reduces radio emissions — it stops automatic broadcasting of your location, device info, and AI responses over the mesh. You can still send and receive messages manually. To fully pause all radio communication, use Radio Silence in the COMMS panel → Broadcast Settings.

PASSWORD & ENCRYPTION

The master password is optional. You’ll be prompted to create one the first time you enable encryption — either by turning on "Encrypt Chat History" in Settings, or by uploading a file to the Locked tab in Workspace. This one password protects everything that is encrypted:

Encrypted chatsYour conversations are scrambled so only you can read them
Locked filesFiles you upload to the "Locked" tab in the Workspace
Exported backupsConversation exports are encrypted too

Changing your password:
Go to Settings → System & Data → Change Master Password. This re-encrypts all your data with the new password. Nothing is deleted — your data stays intact.

Forgot your password:
Click "Forgot password?" when you're asked to enter it. ⚠ Warning: This permanently deletes ALL encrypted conversations and locked files. BEACON, the Library, Prompts, unlocked files, and your settings are not affected. There is no way to recover a forgotten password — this is by design for your security.

MESH COMMS

Send and receive encrypted text messages over radio — no cell service, no internet, no monthly fees. Your Blackout Drive is one station on the mesh. Anyone with a Meshtastic radio can join — there's no limit to how many.

Your setup Plug a Meshtastic radio into your computer via USB. Open the COMMS panel — the drive detects it automatically.
Other people Each person needs a radio paired to their phone via Bluetooth, plus the free Meshtastic app (App Store / Google Play). No limit on group size.
Channels Group conversations. All radios start on the same default channel (CH 0). To create a private encrypted channel, use Mesh Provisioning in the COMMS panel (click the 📡 button) and share the QR code with your group.
Direct messages Click any person's name in the sidebar to send them a private message. DMs are encrypted and don't appear in the group feed.
Encryption 🔒 = channel encrypted (everyone on the channel can read it). 🔐 = direct message (encrypted). CH 0 uses a default key that all Meshtastic radios share — provision a private channel for real privacy.
@beacon Leave the drive running with a radio. Anyone on the mesh can text "@beacon" followed by a question. The AI answers over radio.
Range 1–10+ miles depending on terrain. More radios in between = longer range (messages hop radio to radio).
Radio Silence Blocks all outbound radio transmissions and AI dispatch. The radio hardware may still passively receive RF packets. For true zero-emission security, physically disconnect the radio. Found in COMMS → Broadcast Settings. Separate from Blackout Protocol (which reduces radio emissions but doesn't block manual messaging).

COMMS is built on Meshtastic (meshtastic.org), a free open-source mesh networking protocol. The Meshtastic app is free on iOS and Android.
